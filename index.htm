USA PvE Guide for Hearts of Iron IV: Learning to Fight With Two Swords

This guide covers everything you need to build up the USA for a fight against Japan and the Axis by 1941 in a historical game against the AI. Most of the strategy can be carried over to a multiplayer game but remember that a human will fight much differently than the AI so certain aspects of the research, and the resulting Army, Airforce, and Naval compositions, will need to be adjusted accordingly when facing a human opponent. Also, this guide assumes you have the Man the Guns and No Step Back expansions.

The United States has a huge foucs tree and just looking at it can leave one feeling overwhelmed. Don't worry, the proper path is pretty simple and will initially focus on removing the Great Depression debuff and getting you off of the Undisturbed Isolation economy law as quickly as possible. Then the path will load you up with buffs in the form of national spirits that will allow you to become the most fearsome war machine the world has ever seen.

There are several points where the focus tree is tied to political power actions, with an action needing to be done immetiantly before or after a specific focus is taken. In these situaions this order is very important and often flat out required. When this happens arrows between the Focus Tree path and the Political Power actions will point from the item to be done first to the item to be done second. For example, at the very start of the game Medium Lobbying Effort must be taken before you start Continue the New Deal. Other than when you reach a green arrow indicated order point, the focus tree and political power path will generally be out of sync, this is normal.

If you reach a point in the political power actions where you cannot take the next item due to missing a prerequitie, such as needing to be at war with Germany to take Total Mobilizaton, or needing to complete a focus, then wait till you can take the action in question, do not skip ahead or grab other items.

The research path makes a few assumptions:

  • Your navy will be based around several 1936 carriers and a large escort force.
  • Your convoy raiding will be done mostly by surface ships backed up by your main naval strike force.
  • Your ground army will be based around medium tank divisions for offense and infantry divisions for defense.
  • Your airforce will be focused on fighters and CAS.

You can click on each item to mark it so you don't loose track of where you are.

Start by building max level infrastructure in the following states:

  • New England
  • New York
  • Pennsylvania
  • Ohio
  • Indiana
  • Michigan
  • Illinios
  • Missouri
  • Virginia
  • Maryland
  • New Jersey
  • Texas
  • Calafornia

When the game informs you that you have free civilian factories queue up the maximum possible number of civilian factories in those same states.

Next, build max level infrastructure in the following states:

  • Tennessee
  • Wisconsin
  • Minnesota
  • Utah
  • Nevada
  • Idaho
  • Louisiana
  • Georga
  • Alabama
  • North Carolina
  • South Carolina
  • Florida

Then queue up enough dockyards to give you a total of 80. Remember that the Two Ocean Navy Act Focus will give you 10 for free. You can hover the mouse over your factory count in the main UI (top, just left of center) to see your current dockyard count.

Then queue up 30 synthetic refineries in the states you have built infrastructure in starting with non costal states.

Moving out of the mainland US for a bit build the following on the island of Guam in the Western Pacific:

  • Max level port
  • Level 1 Airfield
  • Max level radar

Now build the following in Mindanao in the southern Philippines:

  • Max level port
  • One level of infrastructure

This will allow you to use these locations as staging points for naval invasions of Saipan, Iow Jima, and the Japanese home islands.

Back on the mainland convert civilian factroies to military factroies in states that have max infrastructure until you have about 50 civilian factories left.

Next build a mix of civilian and military factories until you have about 60 civilian factories to top off for the initial war effort.

As a final step queue up max level infrastructure in all states that don't already have it and then the maximum possible number of military factories in every state.

Past this point you can build a few extra factories in places like Alaska and Hawaii but it doesn't really matter any more.

The US starts off with a powerful navy. Adding three more of the pre-designed Yorktown class carriers will give you the basis for two very powerful strike forces. I usually also build a couple of cruiser deck conversion light carriers for invasion support. But the vast majority of my naval production is focused into Destroyers and "light" Heavy Cruisers. That is, Heavy Cruisers that mount almost exclusively light guns.

These ships are designed to take advantage of four facts about HoI4 naval combat:

  • The higher a ship's speed and the lower its surface visibility, the harder it is to hit.
  • Capital ships that are fully screened get a +40% bonus to accuracy for all of their weapons.
  • Light guns always shoot screening ships first.
  • Unscreened capital ships die very, very, quickly to torpedos.

When combined with the Raiding Fleet designer, Trade Interdiction doctrine, and the Concelement Expert commander skill the result is hilariously one sided battles against any AI navy. An AI Japan with a +2 country strength game rule will often struggle to sink more than one or two of my destroyers while I sink over 75% of the IJN in return over the course of the pacific campaign.

Initial Designs


The Yorktown class is already designed and in prodcution at the start of the game. I add three more of these to the build queue. This gives me eight fleet carriers that will form the cores of my two strike forces. The loadout for all of them is 30 fighters and 30 naval bombers.




The Bouge class is the first ship I design as it does not require any additional research to put together. I make two of these for providing air superiority and CAS support for naval invasions. The loadout for them is 20 fighters and 20 CAS.



Early Designs


Around the time the carriers are done building you should be able to start making destroyers and "light" heavy cruisers.

The Mahan is the basic general purpose destroyer. It can patrol, convoy raid, convoy escort, and fight in fleet actions. It also provides excellent AA cover for itself and other ships in the fleet.




The Helena is the intial "light" heavy cruiser. As long as it is properly screened by general purpose destroyers it will absolutly shred enemy destroyers and light cruisers.



Improved Designs


The Benson is a minor upgrade over the Mahan. Sporting a better radar set it is a bit more proficent at patrol, convoy raiding, and convoy escort duties.




The Cleveland is also a minor upgrade with better radar making it better at patroling and convoy raiding.



Final Designs


Moving up to the 1940 tier hull and sporting even better radar the Fletcher represents a major improvment over the Benson in every area.




By moving up to the 1940 tier hull and mounting better radar the Baltimore outclasses the Cleveland in every way.

As the US you really only need two templates, one for attacking and one for defending. If you have the La Resistance expansion set your garrisons to use the cavalry template and forget that the resistance system exists. If you don't have La Resistance... I dunno. Don't pay too much attention to the stats in the images, these were taken in late 1942 and while I was ahead of time in artillery tech I was behind in my doctrine research. Your stats will always be a bit different.


Infantry Division

With a base training time of only 120 days and a fairly low production cost this division is designed to be stamped out in large numbers. Use them to man the front lines and keep the enemy from pushing you back. These are also reasonably effictive at performing amphibious landings.
